# Cat Care Guidelines

## Feeding
Adult cats typically eat 2 meals a day, about 5 minutes each. High priority -
never skip. Kittens under 6 months may need 3-4 smaller meals a day.

## Litter box
Scooping/cleaning the litter box takes about 10 minutes and should happen
daily. Medium-to-high priority: cats are prone to avoiding a dirty box, which
can lead to accidents elsewhere in the home.

## Medication
Same as dogs: any prescribed medication (including flea/tick prevention) is
always high priority, typically a 5 minute task.

## Grooming
Brushing: 10-15 minutes. Short-haired cats need this less often (weekly,
low priority); long-haired breeds need it more often (every 1-2 days, medium
priority) to prevent mats.
Nail trimming: 10 minutes, medium priority, every 2-3 weeks.
Cats generally do not need baths unless recommended by a vet - treat bathing
as low priority/occasional for cats.

## Enrichment / Play
Laser pointer, wand toys, puzzle feeders: 15-20 minutes, low-to-medium
priority. Indoor cats benefit from daily play to prevent boredom-related
behavior issues, but it is not a hard requirement like feeding or litter.

## Vet & vaccinations
Annual checkups and core vaccinations (rabies, FVRCP) are high priority but
infrequent (yearly) - schedule as a one-off task, not recurring.
